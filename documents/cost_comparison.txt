MSU On-Campus Housing Cost Comparison (per semester, current rates)
Crowd-sourced from billing statements — verify with the official rate sheet.

These are room charges only, per semester, not including the meal plan.

- North Tower (double, hall bath, no AC): ~$3,200  <- cheapest dorm
- Riverside Hall (double, hall bath, AC, free laundry): ~$3,700
- Westfield Suites (suite double, private bath, AC): ~$3,900  <- most expensive standard dorm
- Singles (any building, limited): add ~$1,100 over the double rate

Meal plan (billed separately, required for first-years):
- Unlimited: ~$2,750/semester
- Block 80 (only available to Riverside first-years or any upperclassman): ~$1,900/semester

Rough takeaways students repeat every year:
- North Tower is the cheapest room, but remember it has NO air conditioning. If AC is a must, the cheapest AC option is Riverside, not North Tower.
- The "real" cheapest total for a first-year who wants AC is Riverside on the Block 80 plan, because Riverside is the only AC dorm where a freshman can drop to the smaller meal plan.
- Off-campus can be cheaper per month (Maple Court ~$650/mo) but you add utilities, you sign a 12-month lease (you pay summer months even if you go home), and you lose the meal-plan convenience. Run the full-year math before assuming off-campus saves money.
