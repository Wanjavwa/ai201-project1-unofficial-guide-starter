Off-Campus Review: Maple Court Apartments
Aggregated from r/MapleState off-campus megathread

DO NOT SIGN AT MAPLE COURT WITHOUT READING THIS.

The rent is the cheapest near campus — around $650/month for a shared two-bedroom, utilities not included — and that's the only good thing I can say. The reason it's cheap is that the units have a serious, recurring mold problem. Multiple residents (me included) found black mold in the bathroom and around the window AC units. Management's "fix" is to paint over it.

Maintenance is the other nightmare. My heat went out in January and it took 11 days and three follow-up emails to get someone out. The standard response is "we've put in a ticket." A friend's kitchen sink leaked for two weeks.

The landlord (Cedar Property Group) is also aggressive about security deposits. People report getting almost nothing back with vague "cleaning" and "damage" deductions. Take date-stamped photos of everything on move-in AND move-out, and email them to yourself.

Location is fine — about a 12-minute walk to campus — and the neighborhood is safe enough. But between the mold, the maintenance delays, and the deposit games, most people who lived here say they wouldn't do it again.

Verdict: 1.5/5. Cheap for a reason. If you sign here, document everything.
