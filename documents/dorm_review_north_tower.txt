Dorm Review: North Tower
Compiled from r/MapleState and the class GroupMe

The honest take on North Tower: it's the oldest dorm on campus (built 1968) and it shows. The big dealbreaker for most people is that NORTH TOWER HAS NO AIR CONDITIONING. The first three weeks of fall semester are genuinely miserable — bring a box fan, two if you can. By October it's fine and the radiator heat in winter actually works too well (people open windows in January).

What North Tower gets right is location and price. It's the cheapest dorm on campus and it's a 4-minute walk to the main academic quad and the library, closer than any other dorm. If you have 8am classes you will appreciate this every single day.

Rooms are small doubles with shared hall bathrooms (cleaned daily by staff, actually not bad). Walls are thick concrete so it's quiet — much quieter than the newer buildings. Laundry is in the basement and costs $1.50 per wash and $1.50 per dry, card only.

Who it's for: budget-conscious students, early risers, people who run cold, anyone who values a quiet study environment over amenities. Who should avoid it: anyone who can't sleep when it's hot, and people who want a private or suite bathroom.

Verdict: 3.5/5. You're paying for location, not luxury.
