Housing Selection Part 2: appeals, waitlists, and swaps
Follow-up thread on r/MapleState

So you got a bad time ticket or didn't get the room you wanted. Here's what you can actually do.

WAITLIST: After selection closes, you can join the waitlist for a specific building. It moves more than people expect — a lot of students cancel housing over the summer when plans change (study abroad, going off-campus, transfers). Riverside and Westfield waitlists clear the most because that's where cancellations free up AC rooms. Join the waitlist even if it looks long; check your email all summer because you usually get only 48 hours to claim an offered spot before it passes to the next person.

MEDICAL/ACCOMMODATION APPEALS: If you have a documented medical need (e.g., a heat-sensitive condition that requires AC, or a mobility need requiring a low floor or elevator), that goes through the Disability Resource Office, NOT the housing lottery. Approved accommodations are placed outside the lottery entirely. This is the legitimate way to "skip the line" for an AC room — but it requires real documentation, not just a preference.

ROOM SWAPS: Swap requests open about three weeks into fall semester. You find someone who wants to trade and both submit. It's first-come and limited, so the good swaps go fast.

What does NOT work: emailing ResLife to ask for a better room "just because." They won't override the lottery for preference. Save the appeals process for genuine needs.
