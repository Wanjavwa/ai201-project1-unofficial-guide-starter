Move-In Day Survival Tips (Discord #incoming-freshmen, pinned)

Posted by an RA, collected over a few years:

- You get a 2-hour move-in WINDOW, not "whenever." Check your assigned slot in the portal a week before. If you show up outside your window you may have to wait, and the loading zones get gridlocked.

- Bring a fan. If you're in North Tower this is non-negotiable — there is NO AC and the first weeks are brutal. Even AC dorms (Westfield, Riverside) are worth a small fan for air circulation.

- Elevators are the bottleneck. North Tower has ONE working elevator for the whole building and the line on move-in day is 30-45 minutes. If you're on a low floor, take the stairs. A folding hand truck/dolly is the single best thing you can bring.

- Don't overpack. Most people bring twice what they need. The rooms are small. You can buy bulky stuff (storage bins, fans, mini-fridge) at the store off Route 9 the same weekend.

- Mini-fridges and microwaves: allowed up to 4.5 cubic feet for the fridge. A combo "microfridge" unit is the easiest. Halogen lamps, candles, and space heaters are banned and RAs do check.

- Mattresses are extra-long twin (Twin XL). Regular twin sheets will NOT fit. This trips up someone every single year.

- Get your student ID activated for building/door access BEFORE the weekend if you can — the ID office line on move-in Friday is brutal.
