Q&A: Do I have to buy a meal plan if I live in the dorms?
From the new-student GroupMe, answered by upperclassmen

Short answer: yes, if you're a first-year living on campus you are required to have a meal plan, and the default is the Unlimited plan. There's no opting out as a freshman in a standard dorm.

The exception people don't know about: dorms with kitchen access let you choose a smaller plan. Riverside Hall has a shared kitchen on every floor, so Riverside residents are allowed to pick the cheaper "Block 80" plan (80 meals per semester plus some dining dollars) instead of Unlimited. North Tower and Westfield residents cannot do this their first year — they're stuck with Unlimited.

Sophomores and up living on campus have more flexibility and can choose any plan including the Block 80 or a commuter plan, regardless of building.

If you move off campus you don't need a meal plan at all, though you can buy a voluntary dining-dollars-only plan if you want to eat on campus between classes.

One warning: the Unlimited plan is a lot of money for what most people actually eat. By second semester, plenty of freshmen say they wish they could have downgraded but were locked in. You can't change your plan mid-semester — only between fall and spring.
