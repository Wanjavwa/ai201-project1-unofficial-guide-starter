Dorm Review: Westfield Suites
Compiled student reviews

Westfield Suites is the "nice" dorm. Every unit is suite-style: two double bedrooms sharing a private bathroom and a small common room, so four people per suite with one bathroom you don't share with a whole hall. It has central air conditioning and it's reliable — this is the dorm to fight for if heat is a dealbreaker for you.

The catch is two things: cost and distance. Westfield is the most expensive standard dorm (see the cost comparison post — it's about $700 more per semester than North Tower). And it's on the far west edge of campus, roughly a 15-minute walk to the academic quad, 20+ if it's icy. There's a campus shuttle but it's unreliable in winter.

Westfield tends to fill with sophomores and juniors because first-years rarely get a good enough time ticket to land here, and seniors usually move off-campus by then. The social vibe is quieter and more "adult" than Riverside — people mostly keep to their suites.

Amenities: in-unit AC, private suite bathrooms, a shared kitchen on each floor, free laundry (no card needed), study lounges that are actually quiet.

Verdict: 4/5 if you can afford it and don't mind the walk. The AC and private bathroom are worth a lot.
